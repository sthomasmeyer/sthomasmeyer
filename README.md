### Dictionary.com

**magniloquent** _adjective_ • /[mag-nil-uh-kwuhnt]/

speaking or expressed in a lofty or grandiose style; boastful

Someone who's magniloquent tends to speak in grand, elaborate language, often when simpler words would do just fine. This kind of speech might show up in ceremonial addresses or dramatic storytelling meant to heighten the moment. The word comes from Latin, literally meaning "to speak greatly." Speak with confidence, but watch out for becoming too magniloquent.

> _The governor's magniloquent speech used many fancy words but failed to address the actual problem._


### Britannica

**elusive** _adjective_

Difficult to find, catch, or achieve.

> _The rare bird proved elusive to even the most experienced ornithologists._
>
> _Despite years of dedicated research, a definitive cure for the disease remains elusive._
>
> _Her smile was as elusive as a fleeting dream, appearing and vanishing in an instant._
>

_Similar: evasive, slippery, intangible_  •  _Opposite: obvious, tangible, accessible_

> _"The evidence, however, was so fragmentary, and the witnesses so elusive, that it was impossible to bring the matter home."_
>
> — **Arthur Conan Doyle**, The Hound of the Baskervilles (1902)
